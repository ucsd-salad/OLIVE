Looking at the medical scenario: "Where do you look for signs of severe wasting?"

From the `Patient` structure, the `wasting` field has the comment: "look rapidly at arms, legs, chest to assess wasting (marasmus)". This tells us where to look for signs of severe wasting.

The `wasting` field is used in the `Priority` function, so a patient with visible severe wasting should be triaged as Priority (assuming no emergency signs).

Let me create a patient with wasting and prove the triage assessment is correct.

-- Look rapidly at the arms, legs, and chest to assess wasting (marasmus).

-- A patient with visible severe wasting is triaged as Priority.

def examplePatientWasting : Patient :=
{
  -- Patient presents with visible severe wasting
  -- (assessed by looking at arms, legs, chest)
  wasting := some true,

  -- No emergency signs (all ABCD criteria are false)
  breathing := some true,

  whitePalm := some false,
  poison := some false,

  native_decide
